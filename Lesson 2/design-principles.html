Rule of Third

The Rule of Thirds is one of the fundanental rules of compositons in design. As web developers, we learned and used the Rule of Thirds because it is one of the design principles for a website. It is a layout or grid of nine equal boxes to overlay an image, web page, or painting to make them unique. Just imagine two parallel lines on the layout of a webpage going across horizontally and another two parallel lines going vertically down, overing lapping the two other parallels. On a web page, we have a grid to use the Rule of Thirds to layout the design of our web pages. The reason why web developers uses this rule is to make their websites and web pages present well everyone. For example, Delta, an airline company, used the Rule of Thirds well on their website. The screenshot that I took showed some images of people relaxing or enjoying their vacation. Delta used the Rule of Thirds to make that image fasinating and peaceful for us to enjoy not only the picture, but the website.

Contrast

Samsung

samsung.com

Contrast is one of the compositions of the design principles for designing a webpage. It makes design elements look different from the other design elements. In contrast, there are color factors, size factors, space factors, and style factors. The color factors helps with contrast by constrasting the colors, seperating lights from dark or dark from lights. A popular example is a black element on a white background. Size factors contrast and help creating relationships between elements. It will help the customer to know what to look at or what element is the most important to read. Space factors helps to contrast organize design elements. It helps the customer to have more attention towards the element, when it has spacing in between the elements. Finally, style factors helps to make elements notable in their own styles. It helps the customer to see the difference between the elements shape or size. An example of a website that has good contrast is Samsung. In the screenshot, you can tell it has perfect contrast due to their color, size, spacing, and style.
